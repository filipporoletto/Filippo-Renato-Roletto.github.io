02 / About

Engineer of edges.

I'm drawn to the regimes where the math gets uncomfortable — rarefied gases, hypersonic re-entry, and aerodynamic stability problems where every assumption is on trial.

My work sits at the intersection of high-fidelity CFD and the messy reality of flight hardware. At IRS Stuttgart, I just wrapped six months building an aerodynamic database for the passive recovery of the Ariane 6 upper stage, sweeping DSMC and BGK kinetic simulations across two decades of Knudsen number. Before that, at Policumbent, I co-led an aerodynamics group of ten engineers and watched our wind-tunnel work become two world records in human-powered land speed.

I care about clarity. The best simulation is the one whose results survive being explained, defended, and stress-tested by people who know more than you do.

04 / Projects

Selected work.

ESA · Ariane 6
MSc Thesis · IRS Stuttgart

Aerodynamic Characterisation of Deployable Recovery Concepts for Orbital Rocket Stages

Reusable launchers need a way home. For the Ariane 6 upper stage, that means surviving and stabilising itself through the brutal transition from rarefied flow to continuum aerodynamics — without active control, without propellant, using only geometry.

I ran kinetic simulations in PICLas across two decades of Knudsen number, sweeping DAS opening angles, panel lengths, CoG positions, and angles of attack. The dominant variable turned out to be panel length: a 25% increase yields up to 113% more peak restoring moment.
