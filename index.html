02 / About

Engineer of edges.

I'm drawn to the regimes where the math gets uncomfortable — rarefied gases, hypersonic re-entry, and aerodynamic stability problems where every assumption is on trial.

My work sits at the intersection of high-fidelity CFD and the messy reality of flight hardware. 04 / Thesis

Bringing a rocket stage home.

MSc Thesis · IRS Stuttgart

Aerodynamic Characterisation of Deployable Recovery Concepts for Orbital Rocket Stages

Reusable launchers need a way home. For the Ariane 6 upper stage, that means surviving and stabilising itself through the brutal transition from rarefied flow to continuum aerodynamics — without active control, without propellant, using only geometry. My thesis builds the aerodynamic database that tells the designers which shape works, and why.

I ran kinetic simulations in PICLas across two decades of Knudsen number, sweeping the deployable-stabiliser geometry to find the variables that actually drive passive longitudinal stability. The dominant lever turned out to be panel length: a 25% increase yields up to 113% more peak restoring moment.
